01 — the headline

The pattern isn't auth. It's who's on the other end of the gate.

OAuth2 and plain API keys cover 65 of 100 auth mentions across the sample — Basic auth is essentially gone. The real split isn't auth type, it's self-serve vs. gated: 33 of 98 apps hand out credentials with zero human in the loop, while the rest gate behind manual approval (developer tokens, app review) or an outright sales contract. The most common blocker isn't 'no API' — every app in this sample except two CLI tools has one — it's a manual approval step between signup and production access.
